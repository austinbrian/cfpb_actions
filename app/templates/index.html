Definitions

What is the CFPB?

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) is a regulator that polices companies such as lenders, debt collections agencies, credit reporting agencies, and other companies that engage in consumer finance with members of the public.

What are complaints?

Individuals detail anonymized messages about their interaction with companies that they feel have wronged them. Information includes the company the consumer is complaining about, the product about which they have a complaint (e.g., credit cards, payday loans), and various sub-topics that consumers fill in as a survey when registering their complaint.

What are enforcement actions?

As a regulatory agency, the CFPB can take action against any finance company that it rules takes advantage of consumers. Enforcement actions range from reimbursements of funds to punitive damages in consumersâ€™ favor that can tally in the hundreds of millions of dollars.

Intro to Methodology

There are more than 750,000 public complaints on file in the CFPB complaints database since it began accepting complaints in 2011 and among those more than 150,000 have narrative descriptions. Beyond the narrative and product descriptions, the CFPB reports. The data was accessed and remains up-to-date via an API developed by Socrata for the CFPB.
